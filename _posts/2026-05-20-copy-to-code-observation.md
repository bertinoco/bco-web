---
title: "Copy to code"
date: 2026-05-20
tags: [observations, ux-writing, product]
---

I'm experiencing a shift in my role right now. I think it's interesting enough to share.

I've spent most of my career as a teammate inside larger content and design organizations. For the past three years as an independent consultant, I've been a team of one. That already requires a different kind of ownership. But recently, the work has expanded in ways I didn't fully anticipate.

I'm not just writing copy in Figma anymore. I'm in the codebase. I'm creating branches, committing copy changes, submitting and reviewing pull requests. I can see how strings land in product, how I interact with developer workflows, how content decisions I make upstream affect translation accuracy downstream. The job isn't "writer" in any traditional sense. It's closer to being a systems architect for language quality and consistency, working from inside the product itself.

That's a fundamentally different feeling than being handed a doc and asked to edit a few strings with no visibility into where they go or how they're used. That version of the role doesn't add enough value anymore and is increasingly easy to automate.

For scale-ups especially, I think one embedded person operating this way — with end-to-end visibility and the autonomy to move between design and dev environments — can deliver the kind of consistency and quality that's hard to maintain across a larger, more distributed team.

I feel like a participant in shaping the product, not writing support. It's genuinely rewarding, and it's the most engaged I've felt doing this work in a long time.

Anyone else feeling this way?
